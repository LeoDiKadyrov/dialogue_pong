# Dialogue Pong — Marketing Commitment Card

**Stage:** Pre-PMF
**Date:** 2026-03-26

---

## Audience
20-something who used to use Omegle, misses random stranger connection, is put off by the gross stuff and doesn't want to sit on camera.

**⚠ Risk:** ICP not yet validated. Can't name 5 real people who fit this profile. First 90 days have a second job: find out who actually shows up and plays.

---

## Messages to test (run all three, track which gets response)
1. **Problem-led:** Tired of video chat where nothing happens?
2. **Outcome-led:** You just had a real conversation with a stranger — and you're not sure how it happened.
3. **Difference-led:** The only chat where you can't skip the other person.

---

## Channel
**Primary:** Reddit — r/lonely, r/socialanxiety, r/MakeNewFriendsHere, r/Omegle
**Commitment:** 6 months. No new channels until this one is working.
**Rule:** Participate genuinely. Member of these communities, not a promoter. Drop the link only when it's actually relevant.

---

## Content System
**Type 1 — Proof of work:** Share anonymized dialogue excerpts from real games as they happen.
**Type 2 — Curate + comment:** When someone posts about loneliness or "how do I talk to strangers," respond with genuine insight. Link the game only when it fits.
**Cadence:** 2x per week. Non-negotiable floor. Never skip a week.

---

## Trust Signal
Open-source GitHub repo + public play counter visible in the game (to be added before launch push). Together: proves it's real, built with care, and actually used.

---

## 90-Day Checkpoint
**Working:** 10 organic games played by strangers (not you, wife, or friends)
**Not working:** Adjust message first → then targeting within Reddit → then consider adding itch.io as second channel. Never skip straight to paid.

---

## The Rules
1. No new channels for 6 months
2. 2x/week is the floor — never skip a week
3. No paid spend until organic proves the message
4. Every post is a message test — note what gets replies vs. silence
5. When 10 organic games happen, come back and identify who those players actually were
